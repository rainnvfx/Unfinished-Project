About Kanye

American rapper, record producer, fashion designer

Kanye "Omari" West (born June 8, 1977) is an American rapper, record producer, and fashion designer. He has been influential in the 21st-century development of mainstream hip hop and popular music in general. He has made about 9 studio albums containing 133 released songs. He also owns a sneaker brand called "Yeezy". Yeezy was valued at almost $3 billion in 2019 and West is the sole owner.

Studio Albums

My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy

(November 22, 2010)

Thematically, My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy explores West's status as a celebrity, consumer culture, race, and the idealism of the American Dream. Guest vocalists on the album include Nicki Minaj, Bon Iver, Jay-Z, Pusha T, Rick Ross, Kid Cudi, John Legend, and Raekwon.

808's & Heartbreak

(November 24, 2008)

According to its well-worn inception story, 808s & Heartbreak is part self-examination, part-exorcism of two tragic and life-defining moments for Kanye: the death of his mother, Donda West, on November 10, 2007, from cosmetic surgery complications and the dissolution of his 18-month engagement to Alexis Phifer.

Yeezus

(June 18, 2013)

Yeezus is Kanye at his most Kanye. An electro-dancehall beat poetry album that finds him mixing graphic sex metaphors into bars about race relations in America, its scattershot brilliance would go on to define the latter half of his career.

The Life of Pablo

(February 14, 2016)

Pablo Picasso and Kanye West share many qualities—impatience with formal schooling, insatiable and complicated sexual appetites, a vampiric fascination with beautiful women as muses—but Pablo Picasso was never called an asshole. Kanye, specifically, toasted them. The Life of Pablo's namesake is a provocation, a mystery, a sly acknowledgement of multitudes.

Graduation

(September 11, 2007)

Fame and wealth are the major theme of Graduation, He conveys an ambivalent outlook on his newfound fame and media scrutiny alongside providing inspirational messages of triumph directed at listeners. The album concludes the education theme of West's first two studio albums, The College Dropout (2004) and Late Registration (2005).

Late Registration

(August 30, 2005)

West's lyrics explore both personal and broader political themes, including poverty, drug trafficking, racism, healthcare, and the blood diamond trade. In the first week of release, Late Registration debuted at number one on the US Billboard 200 and sold 860,000 copies.

The College Dropout

(February 10, 2004)

Kanye West’s The College Dropout was one of the most astonishing debut albums in hip-hop history. Multi-faceted, idiosyncratic and packed with enough pop nous to conquer the charts, it brought him both critical acclaim and huge commercial success.

Jesus is King

(October 25, 2019)

On Jesus Is King, West’s relationship to Christianity and the Church is simpler. He’s no longer poking at contradictions, but reveling in a newfound zeal. Though the album is not, strictly stylistically speaking, a gospel record, its steadfast call to worship is its defining feature. The scope here is narrow, a distillation that clearly brings West some measure of comfort, but it comes with a simplification of the music itself.

Ye

(June 1, 2018)

West's bipolar disorder and suicidal thoughts are mentioned on the album, which are in relation to the theme of mental health. Ye also includes West expressing his thoughts towards women.

Brand Yeezy

Adidas Yeezy is a fashion collaboration between German sportswear company Adidas and American designer, rapper, entrepreneur and personality Kanye West. The collaboration has become notable for its high-end limited edition colorways and general releases offered by the Yeezy Boost sneakers line up.
